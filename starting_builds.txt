BUILD 1 — QUIRKY NAME: "The Velvet Rolodex"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Theme: Social butterfly. You know influential people, parties, gossip, friend
circles. You move through rooms better than through spreadsheets.

STARTING STATS (TIER — numbers later)
  • Wealth:        LOW
  • Competence:    LOW
  • Visibility:    HIGH

SHORT TAGLINE (for UI later)
  Famous faces save your DMs. Spreadsheets do not.

SECOND-PERSON BIO (player-facing)
  You basically live in the overlap between "plus-one" and "plus-ten." Celeb
  circles, launch parties, group chats that could move markets if anyone took
  screenshots—you know who to bump into, who owes a favor, and which rumor
  is fashion versus fact. School was… a phase. You are not pretending to be
  a policy wonk; you are naturally good at selling the dream, and your dad
  (and a few friends who should know better) tossed seed money at your firm
  because you are very convincing before the receipts arrive. Execution?
  That is a tomorrow problem. Today you have people knowledge, charm, and a
  phone that never stops buzzing.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BUILD 2 — QUIRKY NAME: "Summa Cum Basement"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Theme: Top-of-class PR brain, zero runway, zero network. Book-smart, basement-
operational.

STARTING STATS (TIER — numbers later)
  • Wealth:        LOW
  • Competence:    HIGH
  • Visibility:    LOW

SHORT TAGLINE (for UI later)
  You wrote the syllabus. You did not write the guest list.

SECOND-PERSON BIO (player-facing)
  You graduated like it was a competitive sport, then started a firm the way
  other people start a side project—except yours has footnotes. You can
  deconstruct a narrative arc before breakfast, but your Rolodex is mostly
  PDFs and one cousin who works in compliance. You are operating out of your
  parents’ basement (you call it "headquarters"; the echo agrees). Money is
  thin; contacts are thinner—just enough here and there to prove you are not
  imaginary. You are very good at the work and slightly haunted by how little
  of the world has met you yet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BUILD 3 — QUIRKY NAME: "The Portfolio Pivot"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Theme: Millionaire operator branching into PR. Cash and hires; instincts still
on training wheels.

STARTING STATS (TIER — numbers later)
  • Wealth:        HIGH
  • Competence:    LOW
  • Visibility:    LOW

SHORT TAGLINE (for UI later)
  You can buy reach. You are still buying vocabulary.

SECOND-PERSON BIO (player-facing)
  You already own enough businesses that your accountant has an accountant.
  Reputation management is the new toy—because diversification is a lifestyle,
  and you get bored easily. You do not know PR the way you know EBITDA, but
  you know one or two genuinely influential people, and you know how to hire
  people who claim they do. Your plan is simple: pay for reach, delegate the
  rest, and try not to confuse "headline" with "strategy" more than twice a
  quarter. Confidence is an asset. Denial is… also present.
